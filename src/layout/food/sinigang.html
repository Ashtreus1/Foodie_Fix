Sinigang na Baboy

Information

Sinigang na Baboy is a traditional Filipino sour soup made with pork, vegetables, and a souring agent. It is known for its tangy and savory flavor, with the sourness coming from tamarind or other sour fruits. This comforting dish is often enjoyed hot with steamed rice.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb pork belly or pork ribs, cut into serving pieces
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 radish, sliced
  • 1 eggplant, sliced
  • 8-10 pieces string beans, cut into 2-inch lengths
  • 2-3 pieces green chili peppers (siling pansigang)
  • 1 packet (about 40g) sinigang sa sampalok mix (tamarind soup base)
  • Fish sauce, salt, and pepper to taste
  • Water
  • Optional: kangkong (water spinach) or other leafy greens

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Pork and Broth:
    • In a large pot, combine the pork, onion, tomatoes, and enough water to cover the meat. Bring to a boil and then simmer until the pork is tender.
  2. Add the Vegetables and Seasoning:
    • Add the radish and eggplant to the pot and cook until slightly softened.
    • Stir in the sinigang sa sampalok mix and season with fish sauce, salt, and pepper to taste. Add the string beans and green chili peppers.
  3. Optional: Add Leafy Greens:
    • Add kangkong or other leafy greens and cook for another minute until wilted.
  4. Serve:
    • Serve hot with steamed rice. Enjoy the sour and savory flavors of this classic Filipino dish!
