Sisig

Information

Sisig is a popular Filipino dish made from chopped, grilled pork parts, typically the head and liver, seasoned with calamansi (Philippine lime) and chili peppers. It is often served on a sizzling plate, topped with a raw egg and garnished with green onions. Sisig is known for its savory, tangy, and slightly spicy flavor, making it a favorite among Filipinos as a main dish or pulutan (appetizer).

Ingredients

  • 1 lb pork face (snout and cheeks), boiled and grilled
  • 1/4 lb pork liver, boiled and grilled
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3-5 pieces red chili peppers (siling labuyo), chopped
  • 2-3 pieces green chili peppers, chopped
  • 3 tbsp calamansi juice
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 raw egg (optional)
  • Green onions, chopped (for garnish)
  • Sizzling plate (for serving)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Pork:
    • Boil the pork face and liver until tender, then grill until crispy. Chop into small pieces.
  2. Saute the Ingredients:
    • In a pan, melt the butter and saut√© the onions until translucent.
    • Add the chopped pork face and liver to the pan. Cook until slightly crispy.
  3. Season the Sisig:
    • Add the red and green chili peppers to the pan. Stir in the calamansi juice and soy sauce. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Cook for another minute.
  4. Serve the Sisig:
    • Transfer the sisig to a sizzling plate. Top with a raw egg (if desired) and garnish with chopped green onions. Serve hot and sizzling, usually with rice or as a pulutan. Enjoy the rich and flavorful taste of this classic Filipino dish!
